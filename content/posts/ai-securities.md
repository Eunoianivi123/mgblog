Hello everyone — this is the 5th post in my “TIL (Today I Learned)” series. Today I want to share a few important security-related concepts in AI.

The newly released models from Anthropic (Fable 5) and OpenAI (GPT-5.6 Saul) are increasingly treating security as a core feature because it is essential for survival in the industry. Guardrails, or safety protocols, along with classifiers that monitor user inputs and outputs in real time, help block harmful behavior. However, they can also slow down performance and sometimes penalize legitimate use cases.

JADEPUFFER is a newly introduced form of agentic ransomware, a malicious program designed to block access to files by encrypting them until a ransom is paid. What makes it more concerning is the speed and scale at which it can operate compared to traditional manual attacks. It is still in the early stages of development, and it lose its encryption key.

CLICKFIX is a new social engineering technique that tricks developers into copying and pasting commands into their terminals. It is similar to phishing emails, but it relies on user trust and urgency instead of traditional email deception. If someone blindly follows the instructions, it can cause serious damage to their system.
